How to read this

Scoring

Where a gauge has usable history, its status comes from the percentile of its own past — 90th percentile or hotter is stretched, 75th–90th is caution. Where history is short (concentration, AAPL P/E) the thresholds are fixed and written into each gauge's note. Regime gauges (yield curve, VIX term structure) use the rules described on their cards.

Freshness

Data refreshes a few times per day from free public sources: FRED, US Treasury, CBOE, NSE index archives, Yahoo Finance, multpl and SlickCharts. When a source is unreachable the last good value is kept and the card wears a STALE badge — a broken API never blanks the page.

What this is not

Not a timing signal and not investment advice. Bubble indicators flag conditions, not dates — markets can stay stretched for years. Inspired by levels.io/bubble-detector, rebuilt for a US + India + FnO book.
